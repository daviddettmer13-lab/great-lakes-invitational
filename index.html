⛳ Tournament Overview

The Great Lakes Invitational is a 4-round team tournament pitting Team Michigan against Team Superior across 4 Michigan golf courses, August 27–29, 2026. Teams consist of 10 players each, determined by draft on August 26.

🏆 Point System

  • Match Win — Team with more holes won in a matchup earns 1 team point
  • Aggregate Holes — Team with most total holes won across all matchups in a round earns 1 team point
  • Closest to the Pin — Winning team of each par 3 CTP earns 0.5 team points (cash prize for the individual)
  • Skins — Team whose player posts the lowest score on any given hole earns 0.5 team points per skin
  • Largest Margin (Round 4 only) — Matchup with the biggest hole differential earns 1 bonus team point

📊 Round Formats

Round 1 — The Crown Golf Club: 2v2 Scramble. All players hit from the tee; the team picks the best shot and everyone plays from that spot, repeating until holed.

5 matchups of 2v2 covering all 10 players per team.

Round 2 — Sugar Loaf Old Course: Stableford Combined. Everyone plays individual stroke play, Stableford points awarded per hole. Team with highest combined total wins the hole.

Triple+: 0pts · Double: 1pt · Bogey: 2pts · Par: 3pts · Birdie: 5pts · Eagle: 7pts

Round 3 — Manitou Passage: Alt-Shot / Scramble Combo. All players hit tee shots → scramble to best drive → alternate shots on approach → scramble putting.

Round 4 — Grand Traverse Resort: Singles Match Play with blind ranking. Players ranked by individual points earned; matchups assigned without knowing your opponent.

🎯 Tiebreaker

If teams are tied at the end of Round 4, the winner is determined by Total Team Holes Won across all rounds (CTP and Skins points excluded from this calculation).

🥇 MVP Award

The player who earned the most individual points for their team across all 4 rounds wins the MVP Award. Points are tracked at the individual level throughout the tournament. The winner receives a cash prize awarded at the trophy ceremony.

📋 General Rules

  • USGA rules apply unless superseded by tournament rules above
  • Out of bounds — stroke and distance
  • Lost ball — stroke and distance or local drop rule (group consensus)
  • Gimmes — within the leather, opponent must concede
  • Winter rules (preferred lies in fairway) — group consensus each day
  • Slow play — please keep pace with group ahead
